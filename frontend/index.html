Where are you applying?

Each country has a unique ATS system. Select yours to get a perfectly tailored resume.

🇺🇸
United States
1 page · No photo · Strict ATS
✓

US resumes are strictly 1 page, keyword-rich, and achievement-focused. ATS systems here are the most demanding — every bullet must start with a strong action verb and include quantifiable results. No photos, no personal details — just skills, impact, and numbers.

1 PageAction VerbsMetrics RequiredNo PhotoKeyword-Dense
🇨🇦
Canada
1–2 pages · No photo · Soft skills valued
✓

Canadian resumes mirror the US style but allow 1–2 pages and value soft skills, teamwork, and community involvement. A Volunteer Experience section is highly valued. Clean formatting, transferable skills, and measurable achievements are key to passing Canadian ATS filters.

1–2 PagesSoft SkillsVolunteer SectionNo PhotoBilingual Friendly
🇮🇳
India
1–2 pages · Photo optional · Career objective
✓

Indian resumes are more flexible and include a Career Objective, Certifications, and Personal Details section. Photos and DOB are still common, especially for freshers. For tech roles like AI/ML, explicitly list all tools and technologies. ATS-friendly formatting with keyword-optimized bullet points is increasingly important.

1–2 PagesCareer ObjectivePhoto OptionalCertificationsAchievements
🇦🇺
Australia
2–3 pages · Detailed · Referees section
✓

Australian resumes are 2–3 pages and more detailed than US ones. Employers value transparency and depth — include specific responsibilities, achievements, and context for every role. A Referees section is expected. Balance detail with clean, ATS-readable formatting and strong keyword matching.
